This guide will walk you through building a completely local, lightning-fast

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 🛠️ WHAT YOU NEED (THE INGREDIENTS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  1x ESP8266 NodeMCU Board (The brains)
2.  1x 4-Channel Relay Module (The muscle)
3.  Jumper wires (Female-to-Female)
4.  A 5V Power Supply (An old phone charger works perfectly)
5.  A Soldering Iron & Solder (See the safety warning below...)

⚠️ SAFETY FIRST (LEARN FROM MY MISTAKES): 
Do not leave your soldering iron on when you aren't actively using it. Unplug it 
immediately. Desk sponges, papers, and your fingers are highly flammable/meltable. 
Save your fingertips; you need them to type the code.

Also, we are dealing with AC Mains Voltage. IF YOU DO NOT KNOW HOW TO SAFELY 
WIRE AC MAINS, STOP AND ASK AN ELECTRICIAN FOR HELP. 

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 🧠 STEP 1: UNDERSTANDING THE "DIVA" PINS 
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The hardest part of this project isn't the code; it's the hardware. 

We want to connect our physical wall switches to the ESP8266 so they sync with 
our web app. We do this by connecting the switch to an input pin and to Ground 
(an "Active-Low" setup). 

But the ESP8266 is a bit of a diva when waking up (booting). If certain pins 
(like D3 or D4) are connected to Ground during a power cut, the board will panic 
and refuse to turn on when the power comes back. 

To bypass this "Boot-State Trap," we are using a very specific set of pins and 
disabling the Serial monitor so we can use the RX and TX pins safely!

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 🔌 STEP 2: THE WIRING (DON'T GET YOUR WIRES CROSSED)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Wire your 4-Channel Relay to the ESP8266 exactly like this:

[ THE OUTPUTS - RELAYS ]
*   Relay 1 (Room Fan)   -> D1 (GPIO 5)
*   Relay 2 (Tube Light) -> D2 (GPIO 4)
*   Relay 3 (Room Bulb)  -> D5 (GPIO 14)
*   Relay 4 (Dining Fan) -> D0 (GPIO 16)

[ THE INPUTS - PHYSICAL WALL SWITCHES ]
Connect one wire from the physical switch to the ESP8266 Pin, and the other 
wire directly to GND. No external resistors needed!

*   Switch 1 (Room Fan)   -> D7 (GPIO 13)
*   Switch 2 (Tube Light) -> TX (GPIO 1)
*   Switch 3 (Room Bulb)  -> RX (GPIO 3)
*   Switch 4 (Dining Fan) -> D4 (GPIO 2) **

** IMPORTANT NOTE ON D4: The Dining Fan switch is on D4. If your power goes out, 
make sure the physical Dining Fan switch is flipped to the "OFF" position, 
otherwise, the system won't boot up when power returns!

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 💻 STEP 3: FLASHING THE CODE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.  Open the `SmartRoomController.ino` file in your Arduino IDE.
2.  Make sure you have the ESP8266 board manager installed.
3.  Select your board (NodeMCU 1.0) and COM port.
4.  Hit the "Upload" button. 

Because we use the RX and TX pins for our physical switches, you CANNOT have 
the switches connected while uploading the code. Unplug the TX/RX wires, upload 
the code, and plug them back in!

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 📱 STEP 4: ENJOY THE MAGIC
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Once powered on, the ESP8266 acts as its own Wi-Fi router.

1.  Open your phone's Wi-Fi settings.
2.  Connect to the network: "My Smart Room" 
    (Password: yourpassword )
3.  A Captive Portal should automatically pop up on your screen. If it doesn't, 
    open a browser and go to `192.168.4.1`.
4.  Start tapping buttons! 

Whenever you press a physical wall switch, the web app will update instantly. 
You now have a premium smart room setup, and you still have money left over for 
snacks.
